Q: What is the highest level of hazard perception?
A. Reactive
B. Proactive
C. Predictive
D. Defensive
Correct: C
Explanation: Predictive hazard perception is the highest level - anticipating hazards before they develop rather than just reacting to them.

Q: At what distance ahead do advanced drivers identify potential hazards?
A. 5-10 seconds
B. 10-15 seconds
C. 15-30 seconds
D. 30-60 seconds
Correct: C
Explanation: Advanced drivers scan 15-30 seconds ahead to identify potential hazards early and plan their responses.

Q: What is commentary driving primarily used for?
A. Entertaining passengers
B. Hazard perception training and professional standard
C. Reporting road defects
D. Practicing radio communication
Correct: B
Explanation: Commentary driving is a training technique where drivers verbalise hazards and their planned responses to develop hazard perception skills.

Q: What is the Level 3 commentary response?
A. What you see
B. What could happen
C. What you do
D. What others are doing
Correct: C
Explanation: Level 3 commentary describes what action you are taking or will take in response to the identified hazard.

Q: What is the hallmark of a professional driver?
A. Speed
B. Smoothness
C. Aggressive overtaking
D. Engine braking
Correct: B
Explanation: Smoothness in control inputs - steering, braking, and acceleration - is the hallmark of a professional driver.

Q: What percentage of fuel can be saved through technique changes alone?
A. 10%
B. 20%
C. 30%
D. 40%
Correct: C
Explanation: Professional eco-driving techniques can reduce fuel consumption by up to 30% without changing routes or vehicles.

Q: What is the correct technique for ABS emergency braking?
A. Pump the brake pedal
B. Apply firm, continuous pressure
C. Brake and steer alternately
D. Release and reapply repeatedly
Correct: B
Explanation: With ABS, apply firm, continuous pressure to the brake pedal and steer as needed - the system will prevent wheel lock.

Q: What is threshold braking?
A. Braking at maximum force before lock-up
B. Braking until wheels lock then releasing
C. Gently applying brakes progressively
D. Using the handbrake for emergencies
Correct: A
Explanation: Threshold braking is applying maximum braking force just before wheel lock-up, providing optimal stopping distance on non-ABS vehicles.

Q: What is the first step in evasive steering?
A. Check mirrors
B. Look immediately where you want to go
C. Sound the horn
D. Brake firmly
Correct: B
Explanation: The first step in evasive steering is to look where you want to go - the vehicle will follow your gaze direction.

Q: What is the standard convoy gap at 80 km/h?
A. 30 metres

Q: What is Position 1 in lane positioning?
A. Left of lane centre, normal driving
B. Centre of lane
C. Right of lane centre
D. Hugging the kerb
Correct: A
Explanation: Position 1 is left of lane centre, the normal driving position that provides best visibility and safety margin.

Q: What is the primary role of the Lead Vehicle in a convoy?
A. Rear security
B. Route management and speed setting
C. Navigation only
D. Communication with control
Correct: B
Explanation: The Lead Vehicle manages the route, sets the pace, and makes decisions that the rest of the convoy follows.

Q: What is the correct radio call format for a route change?
A. Route change now
B. Callsign + junction + instruction
C. New route: [destination]
D. Follow me
Correct: B
Explanation: Standard convoy radio format is: Callsign + junction/location + instruction, e.g., 'Alpha 1, approaching Junction 4, turn left'.

Q: How early should an executive driver arrive at a collection point?
A. 5 minutes
B. 7 minutes
C. 10-15 minutes early
D. Exactly on time
Correct: C
Explanation: Executive drivers should arrive 10-15 minutes early to conduct vehicle checks and be ready before the passenger arrives.

Q: What is the professional standard for in-journey conversation?
A. Engage in friendly conversation

Q: What is the minimum number of vehicles in a standard VVIP convoy?

C. 4

Explanation: A standard VVIP convoy consists of a minimum of 3 vehicles: lead, principal, and follow vehicles.

Q: What is the primary purpose of varying routes in security operations?
A. Explore different scenery
B. Avoid predictability - predictability is a vulnerability
C. Test vehicle performance
D. Train drivers on multiple routes
Correct: B
Explanation: Varying routes prevents adversaries from predicting movements and planning attacks - predictability is a critical security vulnerability.

Q: What is the correct response to a passenger giving a routing instruction?
A. Explain why your route is better
B. Ignore and follow your planned route
C. Say 'Understood' and follow unless safety concern
D. Ask for written confirmation
Correct: C
Explanation: Professional drivers acknowledge instructions with 'Understood' and follow them unless there is a clear safety concern.

Q: What is the standard gap for the Follow vehicle in a VVIP convoy?
A. 10-20m
B. 30-40m
C. 50-60m
D. 100m+
