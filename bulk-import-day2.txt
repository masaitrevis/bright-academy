Q: What is the total stopping distance at 100 km/h on dry roads?
A. 85m
B. 100m
C. 120m
D. 150m
Correct: C
Explanation: At 100 km/h on dry roads, the total stopping distance (reaction + braking) is approximately 120 metres.

Q: What happens to weight distribution when braking?
A. Shifts rearward
B. Shifts forward
C. Remains balanced
D. Shifts to the left
Correct: B
Explanation: During braking, weight transfers forward onto the front wheels, increasing front tyre grip and reducing rear grip.

Q: What is the most common loss of control in FWD vehicles?
A. Oversteer
B. Understeer
C. Snap oversteer
D. Power-on oversteer
Correct: B
Explanation: Front-wheel-drive vehicles typically exhibit understeer (plowing wide) as the most common loss of control.

Q: What is the correct recovery for understeer?
A. Apply more steering input
B. Reduce steering slightly, ease off throttle
C. Brake hard and counter-steer
D. Accelerate to pull the front end in
Correct: B
Explanation: To recover from understeer, reduce steering input slightly and ease off the throttle to allow front tyres to regain grip.

Q: What is the correct recovery for oversteer?
A. Brake hard immediately
B. Counter-steer in direction of slide
C. Steer away from the slide
D. Apply full throttle
Correct: B
Explanation: Oversteer recovery requires counter-steering in the direction of the slide (into the skid) to balance the vehicle.

Q: At what speed does aquaplaning risk increase sharply?
A. 50 km/h
B. 60 km/h
C. 70 km/h
D. 90 km/h
Correct: C
Explanation: Aquaplaning risk increases sharply at speeds above 70 km/h as tyres cannot displace water fast enough to maintain road contact.

Q: What is the first response to aquaplaning?
A. Brake firmly to slow down
B. Steer aggressively to find grip
C. Ease off accelerator, hold steering lightly
D. Accelerate to push through the water
Correct: C
Explanation: When aquaplaning, ease off the accelerator and hold the steering lightly until tyres regain contact with the road surface.

B. Triples

Explanation: Stopping distance quadruples when speed doubles because kinetic energy increases with the square of velocity.
