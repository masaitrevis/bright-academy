Q: What percentage of all road accidents are caused by human error?
A. 45%
B. 60%
C. 90%+
D. 75%
Correct: C
Explanation: Over 90% of road accidents are attributed to human error, highlighting the critical importance of defensive driving training.

Q: According to the Smith System, how far ahead should a driver look at 60 km/h?
A. 100 metres
B. 180 metres
C. 250 metres
D. 400 metres
Correct: C
Explanation: The Smith System recommends looking 12-15 seconds ahead, which translates to approximately 250m at 60 km/h.

Q: How often should a professional driver check their mirrors?
A. Every 2-3 seconds
B. Every 5-8 seconds
C. Every 10-12 seconds
D. Once per minute
Correct: B
Explanation: Professional drivers should check mirrors every 5-8 seconds to maintain full situational awareness.

Q: What is the minimum following gap recommended on dry roads at normal speeds?
A. 1 second
B. 2 seconds
C. 3 seconds
D. 5 seconds
Correct: C
Explanation: A 3-second following gap is the minimum recommended on dry roads to allow adequate reaction and braking time.

Q: After how many consecutive hours awake does driver impairment equal a 0.05% BAC?
A. 10 hours

Q: At what speed does a pedestrian struck have approximately a 90% survival chance?
A. 30 km/h
B. 50 km/h

Q: What does 'SMIDSY' stand for in road safety terminology?
A. Sorry Mate I Didn't See You
B. Safety Means I Drive Smartly Yearly
C. Signal Mirrors Indicate Direction Safely Yesterday

Q: Which of the following is a STATIC hazard?
A. A child running into the road
B. A pothole in the road surface
C. A cyclist changing lanes
D. A vehicle braking suddenly ahead
Correct: B
Explanation: Static hazards are fixed objects or conditions like potholes, parked vehicles, or road signs that don't move.

Q: What is the primary cause of target fixation while driving?
A. Driving too fast for conditions
B. Staring at a hazard rather than scanning away from it
C. Poor mirror adjustment
D. Fatigue and drowsiness
Correct: B
Explanation: Target fixation occurs when a driver stares at a hazard instead of looking where they want to go, often causing them to steer toward it.

Q: In the HALT check before driving, what does the 'H' stand for?

B. Hungry
C. Hydrated
D. Happy
Correct: B
Explanation: HALT stands for Hungry, Angry, Late, Tired - conditions that impair driving performance and should be addressed before driving.

Q: Which of the following is NOT part of the 'W' (Wheels & Tyres) check in POWER?
